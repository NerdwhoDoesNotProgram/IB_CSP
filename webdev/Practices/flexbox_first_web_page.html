Gardening is both an art and a science that helps people connect with nature while developing practical skills. This page explores why gardening matters, how to get started, and important terms every beginner should know.

Why Gardening Matters

Gardening is a meaningful hobby that connects people with nature while offering physical and mental health benefits. Spending time outdoors and caring for plants can reduce stress, improve mood, and encourage activity.

Gardening also supports the environment by helping pollinators like bees and butterflies and reducing the need for store-bought produce.

The Joy of Growing Things

One of the most rewarding parts of gardening is watching something grow from start to finish. Turning a seed into a healthy plant brings a strong sense of accomplishment.

Gardening teaches patience and problem-solving. Not every plant grows perfectly, and learning how to adjust is part of the experience.

How to Start Gardening

If you are new to gardening, starting small is the best way to build confidence.

  • Choose a sunny location (at least six hours of sunlight)
  • Start with easy plants like herbs or lettuce
  • Water regularly and check soil moisture
  • Use basic tools like a trowel and watering can

Learn more: USDA Planting Zones

Watering Wisely

Proper watering is one of the most important parts of gardening.

  • Water early morning or evening
  • Check soil before watering
  • Avoid overwatering
  • Water at the base of plants

Rocks and Mulch

Rocks

  • Pros: Long-lasting, good drainage, low maintenance
  • Cons: Retains heat, does not improve soil

Mulch

  • Pros: Improves soil, holds moisture, regulates temperature
  • Cons: Needs replacement over time

Common Gardening Terms

Plant Types

  • Annual: One growing season
  • Perennial: Returns yearly
  • Shrub: Woody plant with multiple stems

Leaf Types

  • Evergreen: Keeps leaves year-round
  • Deciduous: Loses leaves in winter

Sun Exposure

  • Full sun: 6+ hours
  • Partial sun: 4–6 hours
  • Full shade: Less than 4 hours
