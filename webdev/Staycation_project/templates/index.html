Mount Timpanogos: Utah’s Hidden Adventure

Mount Timpanogos is one of Utah’s most iconic and rewarding outdoor destinations, offering breathtaking alpine views,a panoramic view of the valley and it's cities, diverse trails, and unforgettable experiences. While being an iconic view, it also holds significant historical and cultural value. Visitors from across the country come to hike its scenic paths, explore its caves, and witness stunning locations like Emerald Lake near the summit. Whether you visit in the summer for wildflowers, the fall for vibrant colors, or at sunset for incredible views, Mount Timpanogos is a must-see adventure.

Why Visit Mount Timpanogos?

  • Beautiful alpine views and panoramic scenery
  • Emerald Lake at the summit
  • Access to Timpanogos Cave National Monument
  • Unique seasonal experiences (wildflowers, fall colors, snow)
  • Historic and interesting locations like the old bomber crash site

The summit of the mountain is accessible via a moderate to challenging, 14 mile hike. The summit offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape, as well as a metal shack at the summit in which has a log book, shelter from the elements.

Explore the Views

Mount Timpanogos is home to a semi-permanent ice field, which was once considered the only glacier in Utah. The mountain also has a fascinating history, including a Cold War-era bomber crash site and deep cultural significance in local legends. Exploring beyond the main trail reveals even more hidden beauty and stories.
